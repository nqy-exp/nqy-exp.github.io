When I first built the website, I wasn't in a hurry to design a favicon. On one hand, it was because the overall framework was still under development; on the other hand, I believe a favicon is the most condensed representation of the site: a website's content design, the owner's thoughts, and aesthetic taste can all be reflected in this single image.

It wasn't until yesterday, with the official launch of the first experimental log, that I felt the website was getting on track and needed its own visual anchor.

When I first discussed the design with AI, it suggested using a microscope icon or photos of fibers, but I felt that wasn't right; this does not match how I pictured it.

I remembered the original purpose of my website: on one hand, to host logs of experimental projects driven by curiosity; and on the other, because I don't want to be trapped by a 2D resume, I want to showcase my thoughts and abilities in a more multi-dimensional way.

So, for the first draft of the favicon, I wanted it to look like a meteor streaking from a corner of the frame. This was also intended to express my initial idea. Of course, the color remained my website's theme color—orange.

At first, I tried using AI to generate images, but after several conversations, I found the generated images always struggled to capture that subtle feeling. Additionally, considering copyright issues, I decided to switch to hand-drawing. I thought it would be nothing more than a casual stroke and shouldn't be difficult.

I started with my phone's Notes app, but after a few tries, I just ended up drawing a single line. Although it wasn't the meteor effect, I tried using iPhone's Image Playground to turn it into a meteor, but after several attempts, I still couldn't achieve the desired clean, streaking effect, so I just stuck with that line.

After pushing the update, I realized that because the mark was too thin, the favicon looked like a piece of dust, which really went against my original intention. So, I turned to Procreate for drawing. With the previous experience and a professional drawing tool that allows setting canvas size and brush thickness, I felt this time would definitely work.

I originally intended to keep drawing a diagonal line, but while testing the brushes, I made a couple of random strokes and had a new idea.

The favicon was finally settled on an endless, winding orange line. 

On one hand, this favicon happens to echo my name.

But more importantly, this favicon unexpectedly expresses my thoughts. I believe that for scientific research, the process is more important than the result. <br>
The road is winding and infinite, but most importantly, I am always on the way. <br>
Failing countless times, you just pick yourself up after falling. It doesn't matter if you take detours, as long as you know your direction.

As a poem I really like says: 
> _There is a pleasure in the pathless woods<br>
>—— Lord Byron, Childe Harold's Pilgrimage, CLXXVIII_

I hope I can keep exploring without end and find endless joy within it.

建立网站之处，我并没有着急做图标设计，一方面是因为整体框架尚在完善，另一方面我认为网站图标是一个网站最浓缩的体现：一个网站的内容设计，网站主人的思想以及审美情趣，都可以在这一张图上体现。<br>
直到昨天，随着第一个实验日志（Log）正式上线，我认为网站开始走向正轨，应该有个属于自己的视觉锚点了。<br>
一开始最初与 AI 讨论设计时，它建议使用显微镜图标或是拍摄的纤维图片，但我认为不妥，这不符合我的想法。

我想起了我的网站建立初衷，一方面为了放置自己出于好奇而做的实验项目的日志。当然也有另一个原因，就是我并不希望被一张二维的简历框住，我希望能够更立体地展示我的想法以及能力。

于是，第一稿的网站图标，我希望像是一道流星，从画面一角冲出来。也是希望能够表现出我的这个想法初衷。当然颜色还是我的网站主题色——橙色。

起初，尝试利用AI生成图片，但是对话之后，发现生成的图像总难以捕捉到那种微妙的感觉。再者考虑到版权问题，我决定转向亲手绘制。我本以为这不过是随手一笔的事，应该不难。

一开始用的就是手机备忘录，试了几下之后，就画了一道杠。我还想通过Iphone智能的图乐园来变成流星效果，试了几次还是达不到想要的简洁划过效果，所以还是使用了这道杠。

（图片见英文版：第一版网站图标（使用IOS备忘录绘制））

在推送更新之后，发现由于这个标志过于细了，图标像一根灰尘一样，实在是有违初衷。所以我转向使用Procreate软件进行绘制，有了之前的经验，配合可以设定画布尺寸和笔触粗细的专业绘图软件，这次一定没问题。<br>
我本来还是想要继续画一条斜线，但是在试画笔的时候随手画了两下，有了新的想法。

（图片见英文版：网站图标（使用Procreate绘制））

网站图标最后敲定为一条曲折不尽的橙色线条。<br>
一方面这个图标正好是呼应了我的姓名。<br>
但重要的是，这个图标意外地展现了我的想法。我认为，对于科学研究重要的是过程，而不是结果。<br>
道路是曲折的、是无穷的，但是最重要的是我一直在路上。<br>
失败无数次，跌倒之后再爬起来就好。走弯路也不要紧，知道自己的方向就好。

正如一句我很喜欢的诗：
>_There is a pleasure in the pathless woods<br>
>无路之林，亦有欢乐<br>
>——Lord Byron, Childe Harold's Pilgrimage, CLXXVIII<br>
>（拜伦勋爵，《恰尔德·哈罗德游记》，CLXXVIII）_

希望我能在此中探索不止，乐趣不止。
