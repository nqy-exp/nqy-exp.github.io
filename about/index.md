    <div class="main-text">
      This is my background, though it may not look very impressive.  
  <br><br>

As a child, I dreamed of changing the world. I was deeply attracted by the phrase "Chemistry can change the world," which sparked my interest in this field. During my studies, I found chemistry to be an empirical science filled with variables and unknowns--the way a tiny adjustment in conditions could lead to entirely new phenomena fascinated me.
<br><br>

When choosing my major, I opted for Chemical Engineering over theoretical chemistry because I preferred hands-on experiments. My research eventually focused on nanomaterials during both my bachelor's and master's studies. However, as I progressed, the pressure to obtain degrees began to overshadow my original curiosity. After graduating, I entered the job market, however, the reality was much tougher than I had anticipated. During two and a half years of searching, I faced constant setbacks--hundreds of resumes went unanswered, and most interviews led to silence.
<br><br>

Through these failures, I finally saw myself clearly and rediscovered my original passion. I used to believe in the traditional path: "find a job to make money first, then pursue my dreams." But as time passed and I felt a growing urge to do something meaningful, I decided to stop waiting for opportunities and instead dive directly into what truly interests me. I realized that what I truly love is not a job title, but the process of scientific creation from 0 to 1--the act of building an experimental setup from scratch, designing and testing protocols, observing changes, and iterating through failure. This pure curiosity and drive to explore the unknown is my core motivation.
<br><br>

I began looking for things that spark my curiosity and combined them with my research skills and available resources from daily life to start my own studies. I built this website to document these processes. While a resume is just a static snapshot, this site serves as a dynamic, multi-dimensional representation of who I am. I can't guarantee that these projects will be successful. To me, failure is just a normal part of research, but I believe it is still worth sharing. After all, isn't all human progress built on countless attempts and failures?
<br><br>

I want to thank my family. During my job searching, they encouraged me to pursue what I truly wanted to do and supported me by funding my experimental equipment. I am also grateful for the era of AI. Without it, I could not have built this website so quickly. While many people find it easier to learn by starting from the basics, I learn best through gradual hands-on practice. It was through interacting and collaborating with AI that I was able to master the skills needed to build this site from the ground up.

    </div>

    <div class="main-text">
     这是我的履历，或许它并不算光鲜亮丽。
   <br><br>

小时候，我曾梦想改变世界。那是因为我曾被一句“化学可以改变世界”深深吸引，从此对化学产生了浓厚的兴趣。在学习过程中，我发现化学是一个非常具有经验科学的学科——充满了变数与未知，每一次条件的微调都可能催生全新的现象，这种探索感令我着迷。
<br><br>

在选择专业时，由于比起枯燥的理论推导，我更热爱动手实验，所以我选择了化学工程而非理论化学。随着研究的深入，我对纳米材料产生了浓厚的兴趣，并在本科和硕士阶段都将其作为研究方向。然而，我发现由于过度关注学历获取，我逐渐失去了最初的研究好奇心。在完成学业后，面对职业选择，我决定先进入市场进行尝试。但在长达两年半的求职过程中，我经历了大量的挫败——数百份简历石沉大海，面试也大多没有回音。
<br><br>

我在不断的失败里反而看清了自己，也找回了初心。曾经，我总想着走“先找份工作赚钱，再去实现梦想”的传统路径；但当我意识到时间的流逝和内心那种想要去做真正喜欢的事情的渴望时，我决定不再等待机会，而是直接投身于自己感兴趣的探索中。我真正热爱的并不是一份职业头衔，而是从 0 到 1 的科研创作过程。我想要从头搭建实验流程、设计验证方案、观察现象的变化，并在失败中不断寻找改进的方法。这种出于最本真的对未知事物的好奇与探究热情，才是我最核心的驱动力。
<br><br>

我开始寻找那些能激发我好奇心的事物，并结合我的科研能力与手边的可用之物开启研究。建立这个网站，是为了记录这些过程。如果说简历只是对我个人的一次静态记录，那么这个网站则是向外界展示的一个动态、多维度的自我。我不敢说这些项目一定会取得成功。对我来说，失败才是研究的常态，但我并不认为失败是不值得展示的。人类科技的每一次进步，不就是在无数次的尝试与失败中实现的吗？
<br><br>

感谢我的家人，在我寻找工作期间也鼓励我寻找内心真正想做的事，同时还愿意资助我购买实验设备。也感谢我身处的时代，如果不是有ai，我不会这么快的就能建立起这个网站。对于很多人来说，学习是先从最基础的学起更加容易掌握，但是我更擅长在逐步实践中掌握知识。正是通过与 AI 的交互协作，我才得以快速完成从零到一的搭建过程。
